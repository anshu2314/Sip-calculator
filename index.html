The Ultimate SIP Calculator for Mutual Fund Investors

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is an investment vehicle that allows you to invest fixed amounts at regular intervals in mutual funds. It teaches disciplined savings and utilizes the benefits of compounding.

M = P × [((1 + i)ⁿ - 1) / i] × (1 + i)

How standard SIP calculators work

The formula calculates maturity amount based on monthly inputs (P) compounding monthly (i) for the total installments (n) at the beginning of each period (annuity due). Over 10-20 years, compounding generates substantial wealth relative to the principal.

Calculate Step-Up SIP (Top-Up SIP) Impact

A Step-Up SIP allows you to increase your monthly savings by a fixed percentage every year, usually matching salary growth. This small top-up results in massive differences in the final maturity amount.

Why Step-Up compounding is so powerful

Unlike fixed SIPs, step-up SIPs increase contributions incrementally. For example, a 10% annual top-up on a ₹10,000 monthly SIP over 15 years can more than double the final corpus, helping beat inflation comfortably.

Compounding One-Time (Lumpsum) Investments

A Lumpsum Investment is a single, upfront mutual fund investment. Unlike SIP, the entire capital compiles immediately from day one.

FV = PV × (1 + r)ᵗ

Annual Compounding CAGR (Indian Standard)

To match calculators on platforms like Groww and ET Money, we compound lumpsum investments annually. This represents CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) exactly.

Understanding Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP)

A Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) allows you to withdraw a fixed amount of money from your mutual fund scheme at regular intervals (usually monthly). The remaining balance continues to compound and earn returns.

How SWP works and helps retirees

SWP is highly tax-efficient compared to traditional options like bank FDs or dividends. Since each withdrawal contains both principal and capital gains, only the capital gains portion is taxable. This is ideal for generating a regular monthly pension-like income post-retirement while keeping your wealth growing.

Goal Planning & Inflation Management

Financial goals are target balances (e.g., child education, retirement) that you plan to hit. However, due to inflation, a target of ₹1 Crore today will buy much less in 15 or 20 years.

How the Inflation-Adjusted Target works

When "Adjust target for inflation" is enabled, we compound the target using the inflation rate: Nominal Target = Target × (1 + Inflation Rate)ᵗ. The required SIP is then calculated to match this nominal amount, ensuring your purchasing power is protected.

How to Reach Your First ₹1 Crore

Accumulating your first Crore is a major financial milestone. One of the most famous strategies to achieve this is the 15-15-15 Rule:

  • Invest ₹15,000 per month
  • For a tenure of 15 years
  • At an expected return rate of 15% per annum

Following this rule results in a total investment of ₹27 Lakhs, which compounds into a final maturity corpus of approximately ₹1.02 Crore! You can use our Goal Planner above to customize your own path to ₹1 Crore.

How to Reach Your First $1 Million

Reaching a $1 Million retirement nest egg is a classic milestone for investors. Here is how you can accumulate it using monthly compounding:

  • Invest $500 per month at 10% annual return for 33 years.
  • Invest $1,000 per month at 10% annual return for 23 years.
  • Invest $2,000 per month at 10% annual return for 16 years.

By starting early and maximizing your tax-advantaged accounts (like a 401k or Roth IRA), compounding does the heavy lifting to turn consistent deposits into a $1 Million portfolio.

Guide to Strategic Portfolio Allocation & Rebalancing

A Strategic Portfolio Planner helps investors determine the ideal asset allocation to achieve their target returns while managing risks. Asset allocation is responsible for over 90% of a portfolio's return variability.

The Power of Diversification across Equities, Debt, and Gold

By spreading capital across non-correlated asset classes, you hedge your portfolio. Equity (Large, Mid, and Small Cap) drives long-term wealth growth, Debt provides capital preservation and stable interest, and Gold acts as an inflation-hedge and crisis cushion.

How the Dynamic Interpolation Solver Works

Our recommendation engine uses a mathematical piecewise linear interpolation across six distinct portfolio portfolio anchor points (ranging from a conservative 6% p.a. to an aggressive 16% p.a.). This ensures continuous, non-clashing asset weightings tailored precisely to your specific return expectation.

Capital Protection for Short-Term Horizons

For investment horizons under 3 years, short-term volatility makes equity allocations high-risk. The planner automatically triggers a preservation overlay (90% Debt, 10% Cash) to protect your capital from market shocks.

The Science of Retirement & FIRE Planning

Planning for retirement or Financial Independence, Retire Early (FIRE) involves building a target nest egg that generates stable income indefinitely. It splits into two phases: the Accumulation Phase (compounding savings pre-retirement) and the Drawdown Phase (harvesting income post-retirement).

How Target Corpus is Calculated

We calculate the annual expense at retirement by compounding today's expenses with inflation: Expense_Retire = Current_Expense × (1 + Inflation_Rate)^(Years_To_Retire). The corpus required is then computed using the safe drawdown rate or net post-retirement yield.

The Impact of Inflation on Longevity

Without inflation adjustments, a retirement corpus that seems large today can vanish prematurely. Over a 30-year retirement, 6% annual inflation multiplies the actual nominal expenses by 5.7 times, making active inflation-indexing critical for survival.

Smart Loan & Debt Management Guide

A Loan Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) represents a fixed monthly payment made to a lender to repay both principal and interest. In a reducing balance model, interest is calculated only on the outstanding principal, meaning early payments contain a much higher interest component.

The Power of Prepayments

Prepaying even a small additional amount each month or year directly reduces the outstanding principal balance. Because interest compounds on the remaining balance, every rupee prepaid cuts down future interest charges and shortens the overall loan duration.

Prepayment Strategies

Common prepayments include:
• Ongoing Monthly Prepayment: Adding 5% to 10% extra to your monthly EMI. This is painless and builds a regular debt-reduction routine.
• Annual Prepayments: Making a one-off payment once a year (e.g. using annual bonuses or tax refunds) equivalent to 1 or 2 EMIs.

Guide to Secure Savings Instruments

Secure savings plans like PPF, Fixed Deposits (FD), and Recurring Deposits (RD) are excellent for risk-averse investors seeking guaranteed returns. They differ primarily in investment flexibility, lock-in periods, compounding frequencies, and tax treatment.

Public Provident Fund (PPF)

PPF is a government-backed savings scheme with a 15-year lock-in period. Interest rates are declared quarterly (currently 7.1% p.a.) and compounded annually. It enjoys EEE tax status (exempt on contribution, interest, and maturity) under Section 80C, making it highly tax-efficient.

Fixed Deposit (FD)

A Fixed Deposit allows you to invest a lump-sum amount for a specified tenure (ranging from 7 days to 10 years) at a fixed interest rate. Standard bank FDs compound interest quarterly, yielding a higher effective yield than the nominal rate.

Recurring Deposit (RD)

A Recurring Deposit is ideal for individuals with regular monthly savings. You deposit a fixed monthly amount for a set tenure, and the bank compounds the interest quarterly. RDs help build a discipline of regular savings with zero market risk.

Understanding Old vs. New Tax Regimes

Choosing between the Old and New Tax Regimes depends on your income level and the amount of deductions you claim. The Old Regime offers higher tax rates but allows exemptions like Section 80C, 80D, HRA, and Home Loan Interest. The New Regime offers simplified lower tax rates but removes almost all exemptions, except for a standard deduction of ₹75,000.

Key Highlights of New Regime (FY 2024-25)

• Higher Standard Deduction: Increased to ₹75,000 for salaried individuals.
• Tax-Free Limit u/s 87A: Section 87A rebate makes taxable incomes up to ₹7,00,000 completely tax-free. Marginal relief is also introduced for incomes slightly exceeding ₹7,00,000.
• Lower Slab Tax Rates: Simplified rates (5%, 10%, 15%, 20%, 30%) with slab boundaries optimized for savings.

When is the Old Regime Better?

The Old Regime is usually beneficial for individuals with high investments in tax-saving instruments. If your total exemptions (80C, 80D, HRA, Home Loan Interest, etc.) exceed ₹3.75 Lakhs to ₹4.25 Lakhs (depending on salary bracket), the Old Regime might result in a lower tax liability despite its higher slab rates.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a route offered by mutual funds where you invest fixed amounts at regular intervals (monthly, weekly) instead of one-time deposits. It fosters savings discipline and utilizes rupee cost averaging to buy more units when markets are down and fewer units when markets rise.

A Lumpsum investment represents a single one-time capital deposit compounded continuously. Standard calculators compound lumpsums annually (CAGR), whereas SIP investments compound monthly as new installments are added each month.

Inflation reduces the purchasing power of money over time. A target of ₹1 Crore today will buy significantly less in 15 or 20 years. Adjusting your target for inflation calculates the actual higher nominal amount you must accumulate to maintain your target buying power.

A Step-Up SIP involves increasing your monthly contribution by a fixed percentage (e.g. 5% or 10%) every year, aligning with increments in your annual income. This top-up significantly boosts the final corpus size due to compounding on a growing principal base.
